Théorie du Signe

Jakobson saw beyond the simple view of language as a bound bundle. Under the blind, brute phoneme lies a dyadic structure that grounds all words, all meaning—language. This dyadic structure is the elementary structure of difference, and difference is instantiate the order of logic. Thus we have the differential element of each phoneme, that is, what makes each of them oppose and relate the other, and better than distinctive features, ''differential elements'' capture the qualities of phonemes.
