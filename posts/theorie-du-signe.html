Théorie du Signe

Under the abstract, isolated phoneme lies a dyadic structure that grounds the formation of words, sentences, discourse, meaning. This dyadic structure is the elementary structure of difference, and difference is an instance of the order of logic. Thus we have the differential element of each phoneme, that is, what makes each of them oppose and relate to the other: the distinctive features as also ''differential elements'' capture the qualities of phonemes. This logical order, coming from the patterns of a language, is what makes us superimpose on speech sound matter a dichotomous scale. Jakobson compares this dynamic of superimposition to the diatonic scale upon the sound matter by the musical pattern. There cannot be a separation of the scale from the sound material. This is the phonetics-phonology feedback loop: disctinctive features are abstracted from speech sounds into a system of opposition, the dichotomous structure, or in Saussurean terms: the structure of difference, which will be superimposed upon speech sound matter. It seems to us Saussure's words foresees the developed theory of distinctive features of Jakobson: ''The science of sounds becomes invaluable only when two or more elements are involved in a relationship based upon their inner dependence, for the variations of each element are limited by the variations of the other element or elements ; the single fact that there are two elements calls for a relationship anda rule—and this is quite different from a simple statement. In trying to find a phonological principle, this science is then contradicting itself by showing partiality to isolated sounds''.
